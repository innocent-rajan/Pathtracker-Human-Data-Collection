Purpose of the research: You are being asked to participate in a voluntary Brown University research study to measure human performance in visual recognition tasks. In this experiment, we seek to gain a basic understanding of how the visual system rapidly interprets and organizes visual scenes.

Inclusion criteria: You must be 18 years or older to participate in this experiment. You must have normal or corrected-to-normal vision.

Behavioral Procedures: The task involves viewing images containing letters and classifying them according to rules which will be explained on the next page.

Time required: Less than 15 minutes.

Benefits: Although there are no direct benefits for the participants in the current study, results may improve our knowledge of the how the brain processes visual objects and scenes.

Compensation: You must complete the entire experiment to receive compensation for your participation.

Participant Risks: There are no anticipated risks for the participants in the current study.

Confidentiality: Your participation in this study is confidential. By signing this document, you permit us to use anonymized information for scientific purposes including teaching and/or publication.

Participation and Withdrawal: You do not have to participate, and you are free to withdraw at any time during the course of the study by closing the browser.
